Avoiding Usability and Accessibility problems

  1. Color choice

    Don't choose colors just by your liking. To have a good usability, websites must have a good and compatible color pattern. Pay special attention to background colors and font color. The contrast must be enough to a pleasant reading.

  2. Allways use Semantic tags

    It is very important to use the right tags to display each type of information. For example, titles must use "h" tags, a group of items must use list tags, links must use "a" tag (avoid using images with "on click" solution). This is important because it will help search engines to find your site as also help website readers for people with disabilities. The tag with semantic is by itself a piece of information on your website.

  3. Use conventions

    Conventions are good because they follow good practices and users' customs. For example, login, shopping carts, and information about users' account usually are on the right top of websites, therefore: users will look for it there.

  4. Always use "alt" attribute on your images

    It will increase the efficiency of search engines that try to find images on the web and will help website readers to better describe your website to people with disabilities.

  5. Avoid distractions

    If your website has texts so avoid distracting things such as animated GIF or other elements that can take the attention of the user. Areas for texts must be clean.

  6. Take advantage on CSS

    Use CSS to make your links more distinguishable and attractive. For example, change background colors, font color, and other visual characteristics when the user is with the mouse over the link.

  7. Text formating

    Do not use centralization large blocks of text. Prefer to use left align.

  8. Components' organization

    Do not put your component anywhere. Instead, organize them in vertical and horizontal well-aligned block. Do not put too much space between the components neither put them close together. Find nice space between them.

  9. Fonts

    Do not use lots of font-faces and do not use fonts that are difficult to read.

  10. Repetition

    Do not greatly alter the patterns, layouts, and visuals on your site. Prefer to repeat color schemes, components formats, etc to make everything more familiar to the users. Avoid making each page a new experience to them.
